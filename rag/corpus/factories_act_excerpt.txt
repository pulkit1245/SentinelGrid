Factories Act, 1948 — Selected Provisions

Section 92 — Penalty for contravention of provisions of this Act
Whoever contravenes any of the provisions of this Act or of any rules made thereunder shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to two years or with fine which may extend to one lakh rupees, or with both.

Section 41 — Power to make rules
The State Government may make rules requiring that in any specified factory or class of factories, measures shall be taken to prevent explosion, fire, and compound industrial accidents involving multiple hazard classes.

Section 38 — Safety officers
Every factory employing more than one thousand workers must appoint qualified safety officers responsible for permit-to-work systems and statutory compliance audits.
